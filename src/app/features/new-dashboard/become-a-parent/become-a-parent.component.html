Become a parent and manage other workplaces' data

When you send a request to become a parent workplace, Skills for Care will review it and then send you a notification to tell you whether your request was approved or not.

Skills for Care will contact you if they need more information about your request.

What's a parent workplace?

A parent workplace is a workplace that’s taken responsibility in ASC-WDS for a number of other workplaces as part of a group or organisation.

Parent workplaces can be given edit or view permissions for another workplace’s data in ASC-WDS. Alternatively, they can just be linked to, to show that they’re part of a group or organisation.

Being a parent workplace can help to free up resources. Other workplace managers can then concentrate on tasks away from ASC-WDS, while the parent ensures that their workplaces' data meets Workforce Development Fund requirements.
